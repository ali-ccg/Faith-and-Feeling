Davis Mitchell pressed B-6. The machine took his money and held. He could see what he wanted right through the glass, selected, confirmed, completely unreachable. He spent the next six months figuring out that the glass was him. He was always right there, right behind it. Selected but not arriving.

Kurt Vonnegut wrote in Cat’s Cradle that we live by foma, harmless lies that make us brave, kind, and functional. Davis’s foma was that he was present. That he was there. It worked until a vending machine didn’t, until he found a note in a refrigerator with no date that said: are you there? The conversation you just had is the same question asked a different way. The point was never whether you answered it right. The point is whether you were actually here for it.
